How Can We Help Protect Seed Diversity?

Even as students, we can support seed diversity through school projects, awareness and small actions.

In our school

School garden

  • Grow different varieties of the same crop.
  • Compare growth, colour, taste and harvest time.
  • Record observations in a shared journal or spreadsheet.

Awareness

  • Create posters, presentations or websites (like this one).
  • Organise a small exhibition about seeds and biodiversity.
  • Invite a local farmer, gardener or scientist to speak.

In our community

Local markets

Look for stalls that sell traditional or indigenous varieties. Asking sellers about where their seeds come from can also raise awareness.

Seed sharing

Where possible and allowed, families and neighbours can share seeds and stories about how they grow them. This keeps both seeds and knowledge alive.
