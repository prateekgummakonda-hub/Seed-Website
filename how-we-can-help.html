How We Can Help Protect Seed Diversity

Individual actions

Grow diverse plants

Even a small balcony or school garden can include different vegetables, herbs or flowers grown from diverse seeds.

Save and share seeds

Collecting seeds from healthy plants and sharing them with friends or local groups keeps varieties in circulation.

School and community actions

Seed diversity exhibitions

Schools can organise displays, posters and websites to educate others about local crops and traditional varieties.

Connect with farmers

Inviting local farmers to share their experience with seeds helps students understand real-world challenges and solutions.

Policy and global actions

Governments, NGOs and international organisations can support seed banks, community seed libraries and fair rules that protect farmers and biodiversity.
