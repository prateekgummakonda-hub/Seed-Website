What is Seed Diversity?

Seed diversity refers to the variety of different seeds found within crops and plant species. It includes differences in color, size, genetics, taste, nutrition and resilience.

Why does seed diversity exist?

Over thousands of years, farmers and nature created many varieties of crops adapted to different climates, soils and diseases. These differences protect our food system.

Examples of diversity in crops

  • Different types of rice (aromatic, red, black, drought-resistant)
  • Multiple varieties of wheat grown in different regions
  • Traditional millets adapted to dry areas
  • Tomatoes with different shapes, colors and nutrients
