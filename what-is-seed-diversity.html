What is Seed Diversity?

Seed diversity is the variety of different seeds and plant types within a crop or species. It includes colour, shape, genetics, taste, nutrition and resilience.

Genetic diversity inside crops

Within one species

Even inside one crop like rice or wheat, there can be hundreds of varieties. Each variety may be adapted to different soils, climates or uses.

Visible differences

Some seeds are long and thin, others round and small. Colours can be white, red, black or brown. These traits are linked to different genes.

Examples of seed diversity

Rice

Short-grain, long-grain, aromatic, upland, flood-tolerant and more.

Millets

Finger millet, foxtail millet, pearl millet that survive low rainfall.

Vegetables

Tomatoes, brinjals and chillies in many shapes, colours and flavours.
