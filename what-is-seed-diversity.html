What is Seed Diversity?

Seed diversity means having many different varieties of plants, even inside one crop species.

Genetic diversity inside crops

For crops such as rice, wheat, maize, beans or tomatoes, there are many different varieties. Each variety has its own combination of genes. These genes control traits such as:

  • Plant height and leaf shape
  • Seed size, colour and taste
  • How fast the plant grows and when it flowers
  • Resistance to pests, diseases, heat, cold or drought

Examples of seed diversity

Rice

There are thousands of rice varieties. Some grow in deep water, some in dry fields, and some tolerate salty soil. This makes rice farming possible in many environments.

Beans

Beans come in many colours and shapes: spotted, striped, black, red and more. Each type has different nutrients and grows best in different climates.

Tomatoes

Tomatoes can be small like cherries or big and ribbed. They can be yellow, orange, green, purple or red. These differences are also part of seed diversity.

Why this definition matters in science

When we talk about biodiversity, we usually think about different species. Seed diversity focuses on diversity within one species. It is especially important in:

  • Agriculture and food security
  • Conservation biology
  • Plant breeding and genetics
