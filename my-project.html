My Seed Diversity Project

This page explains what I did for this project and what I learned from it. You can edit the text to match your exact work.

Project steps

1. Research

I researched the basic concepts of seed diversity, biodiversity and food security. I collected definitions, examples and diagrams from textbooks and reliable websites.

2. Local examples

I looked for examples of crops and seeds that are common in my region and tried to find out if there are different varieties used by farmers or families.

3. Website creation

I created this website to present my findings in a clear structure: what seed diversity is, why it matters, threats and actions.

4. Reflection

I reflected on how seed diversity connects to climate change, culture and future careers in science, agriculture or environmental studies.

Reflection questions (for my report)

  1. What did I find most interesting or surprising about seed diversity?
  2. Which threats to seed diversity are most serious in my region?
  3. What realistic action could my school start this year?
  4. How does this topic connect to other subjects (biology, geography, humanities)?
