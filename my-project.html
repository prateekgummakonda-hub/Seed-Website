üìö My Seed Diversity Project

This page explains the goal of my project, how I designed the website and tools, and what I learned about both science and coding.

üéØ Project aim

My aim was to create a clear, student-friendly website that explains seed diversity and shows why it is important for food security, climate resilience and culture. I also wanted to build simple interactive tools that make the topic feel real: a seed gallery with filters, a compare tool and a store locator prototype.

üß© Design choices

Layout & navigation

I used a single navigation bar at the top of every page so visitors always know where they are. The pages follow a logical order: first definitions, then why it matters, then threats, actions and finally my project work and tools.

Colours & icons

I chose green tones and simple icons to connect the website to plants and farming without making it visually noisy. Cards and spacing keep the text easy to scan.

üß™ Interactive tools

Seed tools page

I designed the seed tools page to act like a mini "lab" where users can explore different seeds. They can filter by type, climate and colour, compare two seeds side by side and try a simple colour-based image analyser prototype.

Store locator prototype

The store locator uses an interactive map focused on the Hyderabad region. It is a prototype that could be expanded to more regions in the future. The goal is to show that seed diversity is also about real places and people, not just theory.

üîç What I learned

  • Science: I understood how losing local varieties makes our food system more fragile and how traditional crops can help farmers face climate change.
  • Coding: I learned how to structure pages with HTML, style them with CSS, host them using GitHub Pages and add interactivity with JavaScript and libraries like Leaflet.
  • Project skills: I practised planning, testing, debugging and improving the design based on how real users might interact with the site.
