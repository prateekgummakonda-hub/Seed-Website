Why Does Seed Diversity Matter?

Seed diversity is like a safety net for our food system, our climate and our cultures.

Food security

Avoiding total crop failure

If farmers only plant one variety and it is attacked by a new disease or pest, the entire harvest can be lost. With many varieties, at least some will survive and still produce food.

Stable food supply

Different varieties are adapted to different conditions (wet, dry, hot, cold). This means that in changing weather, farmers can still harvest something.

Climate resilience

As the climate changes, farmers face more extreme weather. Seed diversity gives them options:

  • Drought-tolerant varieties for dry years
  • Flood-tolerant varieties for heavy rains
  • Heat-tolerant varieties for rising temperatures

Cultural and nutritional value

Many traditional varieties are linked to festivals, recipes and local history. Losing these seeds can also mean losing cultural identity. Nutritionally, diverse diets with different crops are usually healthier than diets based on only a few foods.
