Why Seed Diversity Matters

Seed diversity is not just about plants. It is about climate resilience, food security, culture and future innovation.

Climate resilience

Different stress tolerances

Some varieties tolerate drought, others handle floods or salinity. Having many options allows farmers to choose seeds that match changing local conditions.

Adapting to change

When weather becomes extreme, diverse seeds increase the chance that at least some crops survive. This protects harvests and communities.

Nutrition and culture

Healthy diets

Different crops and varieties provide different vitamins, minerals and fibre. Losing seed diversity can reduce the nutritional quality of our diets.

Traditional foods

Many festivals and recipes are linked to specific local varieties. When those seeds disappear, part of the culture disappears too.

Future innovation

Plant breeders and scientists depend on genetic diversity to develop new climate-smart and disease-resistant crops. Seed diversity today becomes the toolbox for future solutions.
