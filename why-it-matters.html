Why Seed Diversity Matters

Seed diversity protects global food security, culture and the environment.

1. Climate resilience

Different seed varieties tolerate heat, drought, floods, pests and diseases. This helps farmers continue growing food even under extreme weather conditions.

2. Nutrition & health

Diverse crops offer vitamins, minerals, fiber and antioxidants that our bodies need. Losing diversity means losing important nutrients.

3. Cultural importance

Traditional seeds are part of family history, festivals, recipes and community knowledge.

4. Future innovation

Scientists use diverse genes to create new disease-resistant and climate-smart crops.
