Seed Diversity

A school project about protecting plant biodiversity

What is Seed Diversity?

Seed diversity means having many different types of seeds for a crop. For example, there are hundreds of kinds of rice, beans, wheat and vegetables. Each type has its own colour, taste and special traits.

Why is it Important?

  • Food security: different seeds survive different diseases and climates.
  • Climate change: some varieties can handle heat, drought or floods better.
  • Culture: traditional seeds are linked to local recipes and festivals.

Examples of Diverse Seeds

  • Heirloom tomatoes in many shapes and colours.
  • Local rice varieties that grow in salty or flooded fields.
  • Different beans used in traditional dishes in your region.
