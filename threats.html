Threats to Seed Diversity

Several human activities are reducing the number of traditional seed varieties that still exist.

Monoculture and uniform crops

Large-scale farming often uses only a few high-yield varieties over big areas. This can replace local seeds and reduce overall diversity in fields and markets.

Climate change

Extreme heat, floods, irregular rains and storms can wipe out sensitive local varieties before they are safely conserved in seed banks or farms.

Loss of traditional knowledge

When farmers stop saving their own seeds, or when younger generations move away from agriculture, knowledge about local varieties and their uses can disappear.

Pollution and soil degradation

Poor soil health, chemical pollution, erosion and overuse of fertilisers all affect seed quality and plant survival, making it harder for diverse varieties to grow well.
