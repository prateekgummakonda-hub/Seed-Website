Threats to Seed Diversity

Many traditional seed varieties are disappearing because of changes in farming, climate and economics.

Main threats

Monoculture

Large farms often grow only one or two high-yield varieties. Over time, this replaces many local varieties and reduces diversity.

Climate change

Extreme weather events can wipe out rare local varieties if they are not protected in seed banks or grown in multiple places.

Loss of knowledge

When younger generations stop farming or move to cities, traditional knowledge about saving and using seeds can disappear.

Comparing local vs. industrial systems

Aspect Local/Traditional farming Industrial/Monoculture farming
Number of varieties Many local varieties Few commercial varieties
Seed source Saved and exchanged by farmers Bought from companies
Risk Risk is spread across varieties High risk if one variety fails
